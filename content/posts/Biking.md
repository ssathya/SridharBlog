## About my renewed passion

I used to enjoy biking a lot during my college days, especially until I got a motorcycle. Recently, I've rekindled my love for biking, and it feels so great to reconnect with it! First, let's talk about my equipment. I ride an affordable bike, so I usually go to places nearby where I can bring it. It's an e-bike, which really helps me keep going, even when I feel tired. It's such a joyful way to stay active and enjoy the outdoors! 

Now that I've documented my bike, you will understand why I picked the following routes to ride. All of them are of easy to moderate difficulty. Not anything that is physically challenging. 

My first ride for the season was at [Duke Farm](https://www.dukefarms.org/). It offers over 18 miles of scenic trails through meadows, woodlands, lakes, etc. I should agree, I've never explored the entire 18 miles, as some of the roads aren't accessible to the public most of the time.

I will reference the [D&R Canal Trail](https://dandrcanal.org/trails) repeatedly in this document. This canal spans across New Jersey and extends approximately 70 miles. Cycling along the D&R Canal offers one of the top riding experiences in central Jersey. For those interested, it is possible to cycle the entire 70-mile length. However, there are multiple trails/loops along this canal, and my plan is to explore each and every one of them. 

My first ride was around the [Six Mile Run Reservoir Site](https://dandrcanal.org/images/maps/SixMileRun_map.pdf). If you would see the map, my ride was along the red trail (Creak Trail).

We did a ride in the [Loantaka Brook Reservation](https://www.morrisparks.net/parks_trails/loantaka-brook-reservation/). The park offers a paved path that is around five miles long. The park spans over 850 acres and features almost 10 miles of trails. However, as mentioned earlier, I checked out only the paved path(s), which is around 5 miles. The trails are multifunctional, and you'll see people cycling, jogging, hiking, horseback riding, and more.

Next, I had the pleasure of exploring one of the beautiful [Loop Trails](https://dandrcanal.org/images/maps/Canal_PanelCard_Map_2021.pdf). I enjoyed a lovely, shorter bike ride from Stockton to Lambertville. It was truly one of the most scenic routes, offering breathtaking views. Although it's a brief journey, there's a special section where you'll ride right between the canal and the Raritan River, making it even more memorable. Both Stockton and Lambertville are charming, welcoming villages that visitors love to explore.

I enjoy biking along the D&R Canal because the route follows an active canal, away from traffic, with shaded woods and a peaceful environment. That's why I return to this canal for another ride, starting from the intersection of Manville Causeway and Weston Canal Rd. 

I mentioned the Loop Trails before. If you look at that map, my next adventure was from Frenchtown to Bulls Island, then to Lumberville, Uhlerstown, and back to Frenchtown. It was about 19 miles, and my bike wasn't too happy about it. Since I was riding on a gravel road, I had to use the battery more often. My bike is rated to _go up to 20 miles_ on a full charge, but it almost ran out of power halfway through, so I had to pedal the remaining 7 to 9 miles. The biking path in NJ was quite good, but in Pennsylvania, it wasn’t as pleasant. The bike path in PA was barely six inches wide, so I had to keep my focus on the road the whole time.

In early May 2026, we had an unusually hot day in the mid-northeast USA and took the opportunity to explore the [bike trail](https://www.traillink.com/trail/sandy-hook-multi-use-pathway/) at [Sandy Hook Beach](https://www.nps.gov/gate/planyourvisit/sandy-hook.htm). This multi-use trail spans about 8 to 9 miles along the scenic beaches. However, on our visit, we only rode roughly 5 miles due to construction in some parts of the island.

### Columbia Trail

[![PXL-20260513-184338645-2.jpg](https://i.postimg.cc/jj7ZK9HF/PXL-20260513-184338645-2.jpg)](https://postimg.cc/cKdQBF5R)

This time, I didn't complete the entire trail. It's a pretty manageable ride, about 15 miles each way. We chose to split it into two parts, starting with the northern section to make the adventure even more enjoyable. First, we visited a [coffee shop](https://thecoffeepotter.com/) that is across the trail. It's a cozy coffee shop with a wonderful atmosphere.  As shown in the picture, the trail passes through mature deciduous and evergreen forests that form a canopy blocking the sun. We biked south and stopped when we reached farm and pastureland. I'm told the southern part of the trail is more scenic. I need to explore it soon.

| **Greenwood Lake Loop** | North | 16.8 miles | Loop | Users rave about the stunning lakeside vistas and mountain backdrops, though they note some tight road shoulders. Insight: Incredibly beautiful but requires caution on the steep descents and shared-road sections. | ~1 hour via I-287 N |
| **Henry Hudson Trail** | Central/North | ~24 miles | Point-to-Point | Often split into a North (shore) and South (woods) leg. Many users highlight the Atlantic Highlands section for bay views. | **Jersey City to Hoboken** | North | 14 miles | Loop | Users love the unparalleled views of the NYC skyline and the vibrant, bustling waterfront atmosphere. | **Last Covered Bridge Ride** | Central | 47.6 miles | Loop | Highly rated for its peaceful countryside vibe and passing the historic Green Sergeant’s Covered Bridge. Insight: A fantastic endurance ride through rural Hunterdon County with rolling, pastoral terrain. | ~40 mins via US-202 S |

| **Loantaka Brook Reservation** | North | ~8 miles | Point-to-Point / Loop | Described as having paved forest trails that are busier near the trailheads but quiet down further south. Insight: Perfect for a quick shake-out ride; the smooth pavement is high-quality. | ~25 mins via I-287 N |
| **Middlesex Greenway** | Central | ~3.5 miles | Point-to-Point | A hidden gem that is fully paved and connects Metuchen, Edison, and Woodbridge. Insight: Short but very well-maintained and entirely separated from traffic. | ~25 mins via I-287 S to Metuchen |
| **Old Mine Road Ride** | North | 43.2 miles | Point-to-Point | Users mention it feels like stepping back in time with its heavy forest, historic markers, and secluded Delaware River views. Insight: Very remote with spotty cell service; bring extra tubes and water for the older, rougher pavement. | ~1 hour 15 mins via I-80 W |
| **Round Valley Roundabout** | Central | 28.4 miles | Loop | Users warn that the massive hills surrounding the reservoir are absolutely punishing, but the water views make it worth it. Insight: A high-intensity climbing workout; make sure your brakes and climbing gears are well-tuned. | ~20 mins via I-78 W |
| **Saddle River County Park Path** | North | ~6 miles | Point-to-Point | A very popular paved route that connects several parks. Users note it can get crowded on weekends with pedestrians. Insight: Features scenic waterfalls and bridges; best for a leisurely pace rather than high-speed training. | ~50 mins via I-287 N |
| **Sandy Hook Multi-Use Path** | North/Coastal | ~8.5 miles | Loop | Renowned for ocean views and the ability to ride out to the lighthouse. | **Shore: Brick to Sandy Hook** | Central | 36 miles | Point-to-Point | Users love the classic coastal breeze and passing through a variety of iconic Jersey shore towns. Insight: Completely flat but highly exposed to the elements; always check the ocean wind forecast before heading out. | ~1 hour via GSP S |
| **Washington Rock & Roll Tour** | North | 22.4 miles | Loop | Users enjoy the historic stop at Washington Rock and the sweeping, panoramic views of the valley below. Insight: Features some punchy climbs along the Watchung ridge that will give your legs a solid workout. | ~15 mins via US-22 E |
