Medicare is the federal health insurance program for people who are 65+ or younger people with disabilities. It is divided into four parts:

|  Part |  What is it for |
|---|---|
|  Part A |  Covers hospitalization and other medical care |
|  Part B |  Covers medical insurance  |
|  Part C |  It is a private insurance option that covers hospital and medical costs |
|     Part D    |                                     Covers prescription medications.                                     |

To enroll in Medicare, you can sign up online through the Social Security Administration's website or Medicare.gov. The Initial Enrollment Period begins 3 months before you turn 65 and ends 3 months after the month you turn 65. If you miss this period, you may have to pay a penalty. If you are under 65 and have permanent kidney failure or receive Disability benefits, you may also be eligible for Medicare.

### Do your homework before turning 65

People can start exploring their Medicare options in their early 60s, but they should look more closely as soon as they turn 64. Points to remember:

- **Determine your Medicare enrollment window:** This is the seven-month initial enrollment period that begins three months before the month one turns 65 and ends three months after the birthday month. Before this seven-month window opens, decide whether you want to sign up for [original Medicare](https://www.aarp.org/medicare/faq/what-is-original-medicare/), which includes Parts A and B and outpatient services. Many people who have original Medicare also purchase Part D.  Another option is to sign up for  [Medicare Advantage plan](https://www.aarp.org/medicare/faq/what-is-medicare-advantage/), which includes parts A and B and usually Part D.
  
- **If you are not already receiving Social Security:** Whether you have decided to get coverage through original Medicare or Medicare Advantage, you first need to sign up for parts A and B as the foundation for either option.
        - _You are already receiving Social Security retirement benefits:_ You'll be enrolled automatically in parts A and B of Medicare.
          - _You are signing up during your [initial enrollment period:_ If you are in the seven-month window as explained above, do it now.
          -  There are other cases that are not discussed here. I might update it here on a later date.

- **Decide if financial help is needed:** Medicare is not free to those who participate. You're expected to share costs by paying for Part B and D premiums. Sometimes Part A and Medicare Advantage premiums - as well as any deductibles, copayments, or coinsurance for services you use.

  - **Original Medicare** doesn't have a limit on its [out-of-pocket costs](https://www.aarp.org/medicare/faq/medicare-out-of-pocket-maximum/).
  - **Medicare Advantage** plans have out-of-pocket limits. However, we need to pay a premium for Part B, sometimes Part A, and even for the plans themselves, in addition to deductibles, copays, and potentially coinsurance.
  - **Part D prescription drug plan** has its own monthly premiums and often has deductibles as copays
  - To be covered for dental, hearing, and vision, if you choose original Medicare, you'll have to buy separate insurance plans with their own cost-sharing or pay as needed from your savings. Medicare Advantage plans often provide dental, hearing, and vision coverage.

## Coverage and access

### 🏥 Provider choice  
- **Original Medicare** — You can see any doctor or hospital in the U.S. that accepts Medicare, with no network restrictions.   [Medicare](https://www.medicare.gov/basics/get-started-with-medicare/get-more-coverage/your-coverage-options/compare-original-medicare-medicare-advantage)  
- **Medicare Advantage (MA)** — You generally must use in‑network doctors for non‑emergency care. Some plans allow out‑of‑network care at higher cost.   [Medicare](https://www.medicare.gov/basics/get-started-with-medicare/get-more-coverage/your-coverage-options/compare-original-medicare-medicare-advantage)

### 👩‍⚕️ Referrals and authorizations  
- **Original Medicare** — No referrals required to see specialists.   [Medicare](https://www.medicare.gov/basics/get-started-with-medicare/get-more-coverage/your-coverage-options/compare-original-medicare-medicare-advantage)  
- **MA** — Many plans require referrals and prior authorizations for certain services.   [Policy Guide](https://www.policyguide.com/medicare/original-medicare-vs-medicare-advantage/)

## Costs and financial protection

### 💵 Premiums  
- **Original Medicare** — You pay Part B premiums; Part A is usually premium‑free. Drug coverage (Part D) and Medigap are optional add‑ons with separate premiums.  
- **MA** — Often low or even $0 premiums because private plans bundle Part A, Part B, and usually Part D.   [Humana](https://www.humana.com/medicare/medicare-resources/original-medicare-vs-medicare-advantage)

### 🧾 Out‑of‑pocket costs  
- **Original Medicare** — No cap on annual out‑of‑pocket spending unless you buy a Medigap plan. Medigap can greatly reduce cost‑sharing.   [Policy Guide](https://www.policyguide.com/medicare/original-medicare-vs-medicare-advantage/)  
- **MA** — Has an annual out‑of‑pocket maximum, which limits financial risk.   [Policy Guide](https://www.policyguide.com/medicare/original-medicare-vs-medicare-advantage/)

## Prescription drugs (important for someone taking atorvastatin)

### 💊 Drug coverage  
- **Original Medicare** — Requires a separate Part D plan. Atorvastatin is a common generic and is usually on low‑cost tiers.  
- **MA** — Most MA plans include drug coverage (MAPD). Atorvastatin is typically covered with low copays.   [UnitedHealthcare](https://www.uhc.com/news-articles/medicare-articles/medicare-advantage-compare)

For someone with only one inexpensive generic medication, drug costs are usually low under either option.

## Extra benefits

### 🎧 Dental, vision, hearing, fitness  
- **Original Medicare** — Does not include these; you must buy standalone coverage.  
- **MA** — Often includes some or all of these extras.   [The National Council on Aging](https://www.ncoa.org/article/original-medicare-vs-medicare-advantage-whats-the-difference/)

## Travel and lifestyle considerations

### ✈️ Using coverage away from home  
- **Original Medicare** — Works nationwide with any Medicare‑accepting provider.  
- **MA** — Coverage is tied to the plan’s service area except for emergencies. Snowbirds and frequent travelers often prefer Original Medicare.   [Medicare](https://www.medicare.gov/basics/get-started-with-medicare/get-more-coverage/your-coverage-options/compare-original-medicare-medicare-advantage)

## Putting it together for someone in good health on one generic medication

### When Original Medicare tends to fit better  
- You want maximum freedom to choose doctors anywhere.  
- You travel often or live in multiple states.  
- You prefer predictable, low cost‑sharing with a Medigap plan.  
- You’re willing to pay higher monthly premiums for that flexibility.

### When Medicare Advantage tends to fit better  
- You want the lowest possible monthly premium.  
- You’re comfortable staying within a provider network.  
- You like having dental/vision/hearing benefits bundled in.  
- You’re healthy and expect low medical use.
